\section*{Abstract}

%This thesis presents an innovative WebGPU-based approach to harnessing the computing power of a graphics card in web applications. An interactive 3D object rendering window is implemented as a core component, which is an integral part of the wider auction house system --- a platform that allows users to virtually view and manipulate spatial models of the items on display. The paper discusses the architecture of the renderer in detail: from the design of shader pipelines, to asynchronous resource loading, to performance optimisation that allows for smooth navigation in the 3D environment. In addition, the process of integrating the WebGPU module into the existing auction back-end is presented, as well as the results of the tests performed, which confirm a significant improvement in responsiveness and visual quality compared to traditional solutions based on outdated WebGL or outdated Adobe Flash Player.
